Exercise 3:

3D printing is a process of making three dimensional solid objects from a digital file.

This topic is cool because 3d printers can create solid items from a 3D software program with a minimum amount of material used.

It’s fascinating that 3D printing began in the late 1980s which is pretty old and was also known as a stereolithography.

This device was used as an affordable way to recreate prototypes for product development within certain companies and industries. In a modern 3D printer, one designs a 3D model on an ordinary personal computer and connecting it to the printer.

In brief information, the 3D printer process turns a whole object into thousands of tiny slices that makes it from bottom-up, slice by slice at a time.
